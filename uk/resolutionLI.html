Disproof following set of formulas: S = {(q ∧ r) ⇒ p, q ⇒ (r ∧ ¬ p), ¬ ( ¬ r ∨ ¬ ( r ⇒ p)), p ⇒ q} using LI resolution.
- LI resolution is next step in refinement of the linear resolution.
- LI Resolution requires Horn clauses, otherwise it is not complete.

    Horn clauses include:
  • at least one goal, it is a clause that contains only negative literals
  • at least one fact, it is a clause which has only one positive literal
  • next clause must have at most one positive literal, otherwise random amount of negative literals
- Begins with a goal, and cannot use subresults
